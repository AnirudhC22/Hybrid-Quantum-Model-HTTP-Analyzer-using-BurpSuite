Why Quantum ML Is Needed

1. Complementary Decision Boundaries

The VQC operates in a 24 = 16 dimensional Hilbert space. When classical models disagree (confidence < 85%), the quantum circuit uses fundamentally different decision boundaries to resolve the ambiguity.

2. Quantum Kernel Advantage

Angle encoding implicitly computes a quantum kernel function that captures feature interactions classical polynomial/RBF kernels may miss, especially for obfuscated or novel attack patterns.

3. Ensemble Diversity

Classical models (RF, XGB, SVM) share similar inductive biases. The quantum model makes fundamentally different errors — combining them via weighted ensemble reduces correlated misclassifications.

4. Adversarial Robustness

Attackers can reverse-engineer classical decision boundaries. The quantum circuit provides an orthogonal defense that is computationally infeasible to fully characterize and evade.
